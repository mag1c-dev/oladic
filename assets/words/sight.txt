
<DIV class="item"><P class="word"><B>sight </B><I>noun </I> </P>
<P> <SUP> 1 </SUP> <TT> ability to see </TT> </P>
<P> <U> ADJ. </U><B> excellent, good, normal, perfect | defective, failing, poor, weak | long, short </B> </P>
<P> <U> VERB + SIGHT </U><B> have </B><I>She has very little sight in her left eye. </I><B>| lose </B><I>He's lost the sight of one eye. </I><B>| save </B><I>The surgeons battled to save her sight. </I> </P>
<P> <U> SIGHT + VERB </U><B> deteriorate, fail, go </B><I>I think my sight is beginning to go. </I> </P>
<P> <U> SIGHT + NOUN </U><B> test | defects, problems </B> </P>
<P> <U> PHRASES </U><B> the/your sense of sight </B> </P>
<P> <SUP> 2 </SUP> <TT> act/moment of seeing sth </TT> </P>
<P> <U> VERB + SIGHT </U><B> catch, get, have </B><I>She suddenly caught sight of the look on her mother's face. We will soon get our first sight of the Statue of Liberty. </I><B>| keep </B><I>She kept sight of him in her mirror. </I><B>| lose </B> </P>
<P> <U> PREP. </U><B> at the ~ (of) </B><I>Her knees went weak at the sight of him. </I> </P>
<P> <U> PHRASES </U><B> at first sight </B><I>He looked at first sight like an English tourist. </I><B>| cannot stand/bear the sight of sth </B>(= hate seeing sb/sth) <I>I never could stand the sight of blood. </I><B>| a clear sight of sth </B><I>He didn't shoot until he had a clear sight of the goal. </I><B>| know sb by sight </B>(= to recognize sb without knowing them well), <B>the mere/very sight of sb/sth </B><I>The mere sight of her sitting there made his heart beat faster. </I><B>| shoot (sb) on sight </B>(= to shoot sb immediately you see them) <I>Soldiers have been ordered to shoot looters on sight. </I><B>| sick of the sight of sb/sth </B><I>We've shared an office for too long and we're sick of the sight of each other. </I> </P>
<P> <SUP> 3 </SUP> <TT> position where sth can be seen </TT> </P>
<P> <U> VERB + SIGHT </U><B> come into </B><I>Then the towers of the castle came into sight. </I><B>| disappear from, vanish from </B><I>She watched until the car disappeared from sight. </I><B>| hide (sth) from, remove sth from </B><I>I hid the papers from sight. </I><B>| keep sth in </B><I>Keep their car in sight for as long as you can. </I> </P>
<P> <U> PREP. </U><B> in ~ </B><I>(often figurative) The end is in sight </I>(= will happen soon)</B><I>. </I><B>| out of ~ </B><I>He kept out of sight behind a pillar. </I><B>| within ~ of </B><I>The cricket ground was situated within sight of both village pubs. </I> </P>
<P> <U> PHRASES </U><B> come in sight of sb/sth </B><I>At last we came in sight of a few houses. </I><B>| in full sight of sb </B><I>He tried to break into a car in full sight of a policeman. </I><B>| in plain sight </B><I>They waited until the enemy was in plain sight. </I><B>| your line of sight </B><I>She was now standing just out of his line of sight. </I><B>| not let sb/sth out of your sight </B><I>Whatever you do, don't let them out of your sight! </I> </P>

<P> <U> ADJ. </U><B> common, regular | bizarre, rare, strange, unexpected, unfamiliar, unlikely, unusual | awe-inspiring, awesome, beautiful, breathtaking, fine, impressive, inspiring, magnificent, spectacular, splendid, unforgettable, wonderful | depressing, pathetic, pitiful, sad, sorry </B><I>He really did look a sorry sight, his hair tangled and his clothing covered in feathers. </I><B>| ghastly, gruesome, horrifying, terrible, terrifying </B> </P>
