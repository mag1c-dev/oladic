
<DIV class="item"><P class="word"><B>pretext </B><I>noun </I> </P>

<P> <U> PREP. </U><B> on a/the ~ </B><I>He disappeared into his study on the pretext that he had work to do there. </I><B>| under a/the ~ </B><I>Under the pretext of checking her identity, the man had copied down her credit card details. </I><B>| ~ for </B><I>He used his research as a pretext for travelling to Hungary. </I> </P>
<P> <U> PHRASES </U><B> at/on the slightest pretext </B><I>He keeps popping into my office on the slightest pretext. </I> </P>
