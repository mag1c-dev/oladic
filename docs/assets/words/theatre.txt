<P> <U> VERB + THEATRE </U><B> go to, visit </B><I>I haven't been to the theatre for ages. </I> </P>
<P> <U> THEATRE + NOUN </U><B> ticket | production | audience | manager, staff </B> </P>
<P> <U> PREP. </U><B> at a/the ~ </B><I>We were at the theatre last night. </I><B>| in a/the ~ </B><I>There's a bar in the theatre. </I> </P>

<P> <U> ADJ. </U><B> good, great </B><I>He writes the sort of dialogue that makes great theatre. </I><B>| live | amateur | commercial, professional | fringe, provincial, repertory </B><I>There was some good fringe theatre at the festival. </I><B>| classical, contemporary, experimental, modern, music/musical, popular | street </B><I>We saw some good street theatre while we were in Paris. </I><B>| community, youth | political </B><I>(often figurative) The Chancellor's speech was an absorbing piece of political theatre. </I> </P>
